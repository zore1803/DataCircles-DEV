## Purpose

Manages subscriptions, trials, plans, add-ons, coupons, referrals, rewards,
upgrades, downgrades, renewals, and payments — for CRM organizations.

The architecture is centered on **business events**, not transport events.
Controllers, webhooks, and frontend requests are *transport*. Business logic
belongs in services/settlement.

## Architecture Goals

The billing system is designed to satisfy the following engineering goals.
Read these first — they explain *why* the rest of the design looks the way it does:

- **Deterministic billing behavior regardless of payment confirmation path.** The same outcome whether Razorpay confirms via `payment.captured`, `subscription.charged`, or the client `verifyPayment`. (This is not aspirational — it's the direct lesson of the settlement-race bug; see `flows/Settlement.md`.)
- **Clear separation between transport events and business events.** See "Business Event Philosophy" below.
- **Explicit state transitions for every billing entity.** Illegal transitions should be impossible, not merely avoided (see `state-machines/`).
- **Extensible pricing through independent pricing modifiers.** Coupons and referral rewards are both "modifiers" resolved into one shape; adding a new discount type shouldn't touch the pricing engine.
- **Complete auditability through immutable Billing Events.** Every meaningful change leaves an append-only record.
- **Safe handling of retries, duplicate webhooks, and concurrent operations.** Idempotency and atomic guards, not hope.
- **Business logic that can evolve without changing payment-provider integrations.** Swapping/adding a provider should not require rewriting referral, coupon, or reward logic.

## Guiding principles (enforced today unless noted)

- One `Organization` owns one `Subscription` (the current billing contract).
- Pricing is computed from independent **modifiers** (coupon, referral reward) by a **pure** engine.
- **Settlement is centralized** — all first-payment side-effects run in `runFirstPaymentSettlement()`; never attached to individual Razorpay transport events.
- **Settlement owns canonical state.** Initiation only records intent.
- **`Reward` is immutable** (only `revokedAt`); availability is *derived* from `RewardUsage`.
- Every important action **should** produce exactly one `BillingEvent` (audit: verify no gaps/dupes).
- Idempotency wherever a webhook can retry or race.
- ⚠ *Aspirational / partial:* "controllers contain no business logic" — **not true today**; much billing logic lives in `subscriptionController.js`. Service extraction is an audit goal, not current state.

## Business Event Philosophy

This is the single most important architectural decision in the system.

The billing system intentionally separates **transport events** from
**business events**.

**Transport events** are technical notifications generated by external systems
or API requests. They describe *how* we were told something happened:

- Razorpay `payment.captured`
- Razorpay `subscription.charged`
- Razorpay `subscription.authenticated` / `subscription.activated`
- Frontend `verifyPayment` (client callback)
- REST API requests

**Business events** represent meaningful changes in the billing domain. They
describe *what* actually happened:

- First Payment Confirmed
- Subscription Activated
- Upgrade Completed
- Reward Consumed
- Coupon Redeemed

**Business logic executes only in response to business events**, never directly
inside a transport handler. Multiple transport events can map to one business
event (five Razorpay paths → one "first payment confirmed"), so attaching logic
to individual transport handlers guarantees duplication and ordering-dependent
bugs. This is exactly what caused the settlement-race bug (referral qualified
only when certain webhooks arrived first). The fix — `runFirstPaymentSettlement`
as the single business-event handler — is the concrete embodiment of this
philosophy. Preserve it.

## Core domain model (current)

### Organization
The customer workspace and the top-level billing tenant. It owns at most one
active `Subscription`, many `Users`, and its full `BillingEvent` history.
Almost every billing decision is scoped to an organization.

### Subscription
The organization's active billing contract, and the **central entity** of the
system. It tracks the current plan, billing cycle, subscription status (two
fields — see below), renewal dates, `activeAddons`, trial metadata, the
`razorpaySubscriptionId`, and any **in-flight** one-time operations
(`pendingPlanChange` for upgrades, `pendingAddonAddition` for add-ons). All
recurring billing decisions originate here. It carries two status fields —
`appStatus` (the canonical app-level lifecycle, written via `setAppStatus` with
history) and `status` (mirrors the Razorpay state); their overlap is a known
audit item (`state-machines/Subscription.md`).

### Plans (`PlanConfig`)
`starter`, `growth`, `business`. Each defines base price, feature limits,
included features, and which billing cycles it supports. Plans are the base
layer of every pricing calculation.

### Add-ons (`PlanAddon`)
Independent recurring purchases attached to a subscription (e.g. seats). Bought
via a one-time prorated Order now, then folded into the recurring amount from
the next renewal. Each add-on defines its own per-cycle price and plan
compatibility.

### Coupon
A **recurring** discount definition (applies every cycle), scoped global /
plan-specific / add-on-specific. Validated **fresh at checkout** — coupons have
**no reservation concept** (unlike rewards). Redemption is recorded once a first
payment settles.

### Referral
An invitation record linking a referrer org to a referred org (one referrer per
referred org, permanent). Lifecycle `pending → qualified` (or `expired`). It is
pure *intent* — it never holds money; it produces `Reward`s on qualification.

### Reward
A **one-time** billing credit, and it is **immutable** once created (only
`revokedAt` may later be set). A reward's usability is never a field on the
reward — it is **derived** by scanning its `RewardUsage` rows, which is what
makes concurrent redemption safe.

### RewardUsage
The append-only record of a reward being used, and the concurrency guard.
States `reserved → consumed | released`, protected by a partial unique index so
a reward can have at most one live reservation. This is where "reserve before
charge, consume on settlement, release on abandon/expiry" is enforced.

### BillingEvent
The immutable, append-only audit trail. Every meaningful billing action should
produce exactly one BillingEvent. Real event types are enumerated in
`audit/EventMatrix.md`.

## Pricing (current)

```
Base plan  +  recurring add-ons  −  coupon (recurring)  −  referral reward (ONE-TIME)  =  charge
```
- Coupon = recurring (applies every cycle). Referral reward = one-time (applies to a single Order).
- Arithmetic: `utils/pricingEngine.js` (`buildPricingSnapshot`, `applyModifiers`, `computeGST`) — **pure**.
- Modifier lookup (DB): `utils/modifierResolver.js` normalizes coupon + reward into one `Modifier` shape.

## Payment (current)

- **Razorpay Subscription** — the recurring plan (new subscription, renewals).
- **Razorpay Order** — one-time charges (upgrade proration, add-on purchase). This is where referral rewards are redeemed.
- Five transport confirmation paths → one business event → `runFirstPaymentSettlement()`. See `flows/Settlement.md`.

## Settlement layer

`runFirstPaymentSettlement(subscription)` (in `subscriptionController.js`) is the
single home for first-payment side-effects: `maybeRecordCouponRedemption()` +
`maybeQualifyReferral()` (both idempotent). Future post-payment effects (invoices,
loyalty, notifications) go **here**, not in individual handlers. See `flows/Settlement.md`.

## System Invariants

The following must **always** be true. When debugging, if one of these is
violated, you've found a bug — not an edge case to accommodate:

- Every Organization owns **at most one** active Subscription.
- A Reward can only be consumed through a valid `RewardUsage` (never mutated directly).
- A **consumed** reward can never become available again (terminal state).
- A Reward has at most **one** live `reserved` usage at a time (partial unique index).
- Coupon validation occurs **immediately before** pricing (no stale/reserved coupon state).
- Settlement executes **at most once** for a successful first payment (idempotent across all transport paths).
- Billing Events are **append-only** and never edited or deleted.
- Pricing modifiers **never mutate** base pricing — the pricing engine is pure; discounts are computed, not stored back onto plans/add-ons.
- Referral `pending` visibility never depends on trial/payment status.
- Reward reservations always eventually resolve: `consumed`, or `released` (via abandon / same-flow recycle / 30-min TTL).

> ⚠ Some invariants are *enforced by construction* (unique indexes, atomic
> updates); others are *conventions* the audit must confirm hold at every call
> site. Treat this list as the specification to test against, not proof.
